The Event

Fitness Meets Cultural Heritage

A Run Like No Other

The Jaipur Heritage Run is not just a marathon; it is a scenic journey back in time. As you navigate the cobblestone and asphalt roads of India's famed Pink City, you will be surrounded by century-old architecture, massive historic fort walls, and greeting locals.

Starting under the beautiful early morning glow at Albert Hall Museum, the course takes you through historical city gates, directly past the honeycomb architecture of Hawa Mahal, and culminates at the edge of the quiet Man Sagar Lake, showcasing the floating Jal Mahal palace at sunrise.

Race Start Tips

Warm up thoroughly under the Albert Hall gates. Pace yourself slowly for the first 2 kilometers as crowds clear out.

STARTING GRID 01

Albert Hall Museum

Begin your run at Jaipur's oldest museum. Built in 1887, this architectural masterpiece provides a royal start in the cool morning twilight. Runners gather in organized holding grids classified by target times.

Hydration Point First Aid
CULTURAL CHEER ZONE 02

Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)

Run past the iconic pink sandstone facade. With its 953 micro-windows catching the sunrise, this is the ultimate heritage cheering zone. Rajasthan folk dancers and traditional dhol players will energize you as you fly past.

Hydration Live Music
CHAMPIONSHIP FINISH 03

Jal Mahal (Water Palace)

Cross the finish line right opposite the floating palace in the middle of Man Sagar lake. Receive your royal finisher medal, recover in the sports physiotherapy grids, and celebrate with a hot traditional breakfast.

Runner kits, bib numbers, and dry-fit t-shirts will be distributed at the Bib Expo on 23rd and 24th July at the Sawai Mansingh Stadium. You must carry a copy of your registration confirmation email/SMS and a valid government photo ID. No bibs will be given on race day.

Yes, a secured counter for baggage storage will be available at Albert Hall Museum (start line) starting 4:30 AM. Baggage will be safely transported by our logistics team to the finish line at Jal Mahal, where you can collect it after your run by displaying your bib number.

Late July is during Jaipur's monsoon season. Expected temperatures in the early morning hover around 25°C to 28°C, with high humidity. There is a possibility of light rain, which makes for a pleasant running environment. Water and electrolyte stations will be set up every 1.5 kilometers to ensure hydration.
